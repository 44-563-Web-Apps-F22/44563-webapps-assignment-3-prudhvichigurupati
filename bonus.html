Camebridge Camel

A camel is an is an even-toed ungulate in the genus Camelus that bears distinctive fatty deposits known as "humps" on its back. Camels have long been domesticated and, as livestock, they provide food (milk and meat) and textiles (fiber and felt from hair).

  1. Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.
  2. Some calves are born completely white and turn brown as their adult coat comes in.
  3. There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.
